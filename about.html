About the Creator

William Fuller

Project Creator & Developer, NetVault-Tactical

WF

William Fuller is the creator and lead developer of NetVault-Tactical, a capstone project built to address a persistent and costly gap in tactical network operations: the loss of network infrastructure due to missing or outdated configuration backups.

The project draws on hands-on experience with network administration, cybersecurity fundamentals, and the operational realities of supporting communications in austere, bandwidth-constrained environments.

Our Intent

The Mission

Driven by a commitment to support the warfighter. Maintaining reliable communication for command-and-control (C2) nodes is the lifeline of modern military operations.

The Challenge

Enable units to operate in disconnected, intermittent, and limited (DIL) bandwidth environments. The tools we build need to anticipate connectivity loss, not just react to it.

The Goal

Ensure warfighters never lose their network infrastructure due to a lack of configuration backups — and that recovery, when needed, is fast enough to matter.

Why This Project

Tactical networks are high-value targets. A single unbacked-up router or SATCOM terminal can sever communications across an entire forward operating base, with direct consequences for mission success and personnel safety. NetVault-Tactical was conceived to turn configuration management from a manual, easily-forgotten task into an automated, resilient process — one that works whether or not the network is connected to anything at all.

This site documents the prototype version of the application, including its technical design, benefits, and a full user guide for deployment.
